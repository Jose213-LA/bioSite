Lucia’s Bio

Lucia is a native of El Salvador. She came to the United States because her country was going through a crisis that turned into a civil war. The journey was rough. She had to leave loved ones behind, not knowing what the future would bring, and grieving those who disappeared in the conflict. She made a promise to her younger siblings: as long as she was alive, they would be taken care of.

When she arrived in 1982, she had to quickly adjust to a new life. She settled in a mostly Mexican neighborhood, where the customs and traditions were very different from the ones she grew up with. Still, she adapted, worked hard, and stayed true to her roots. In 1985, Lucia met her future husband Joel, also a Salvadoran who had escaped the war. They married and had two children. Unfortunately, the marriage ended in divorce in 1991.

Lucia raised her two children on her own, working hard to give them a better life and staying strong through it all. She later achieved something she is deeply proud of: building a home for her family back in El Salvador. Now at 80 years old and retired, Lucia enjoys spending time with her friends and family. She stays active in her church, where she helps with community events and uses her talents to give back. One of the ways she’s supported church fundraising is by cooking and selling pupusas, a traditional Salvadoran dish. Her cooking has helped raise money for people in need, showing that even simple skills can make a big impact.

Lucia also enjoys hiking with her family and exploring new places, whether it's a local trail or a cultural site. These outings keep her physically active and mentally refreshed, giving her a chance to connect with both nature and her loved ones. She finds peace and joy in embroidery, especially when creating her own pillow designs. Lucia also knows how to make delicious coffee completely from scratch a skill she learned in the coffee fields of El Salvador, where she picked, dried, roasted, and ground the beans by hand. Lucia lives by the simple values of kindness, patience, and treating others with care. Her story is one of strength, love, and quiet dedication to the people she cherishes most.
